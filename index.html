Books

Mindfulness and self acceptance

  • How to Be Enough: Self Acceptance for Self Critics and Perfectionists: Ellen Hendriksen
    A compassionate, strategy heavy guide for loosening perfectionism and self criticism, aimed at shifting habits of control, comparison, and inner rules toward kinder, more flexible standards.
  • Mindfulness for Beginners: Jon Kabat Zinn
    A practical entry point that frames mindfulness in a secular, health oriented way, closely aligned with the mindset and core skills of MBSR.
  • The Miracle of Mindfulness: Thich Nhat Hanh
    Gentle stories plus simple everyday practices like washing dishes or answering the phone that make mindfulness feel concrete and doable, regardless of religious background.

Performing, stage fright, and mental skills

  • Managing Stage Fright: Julie Jaffee Nagel
    Practical guidance with attention to teacher student dynamics, using that relationship as a model for understanding performance anxiety and the performer’s broader emotional life.
  • Performance Strategies for Musicians: David Buswell
    Stage fright focused mental preparation on resilience, better use of practice time, pre performance routines, and in the moment state management with explicit links to visualization and NLP.
  • Performance Success: Don Greene
    A skill and exercises approach to performing under pressure, working with performance energy so it is channeled into focused, repeatable execution.
  • The Inner Game of Music: Barry Green & W. Timothy Gallwey
    Classic reduce interference framework: strengthen attention and awareness, quiet unhelpful self talk, and keep nerves and self doubt from disrupting what you already know how to do.
  • The Musician’s Way: Gerald Klickstein
    A structured practice to preparation to performance roadmap with explicit attention to the causes of nervousness and how to build more confident, reliable performance habits.

Music psychology, health, and wellbeing

  • Music, Health, and Wellbeing: Raymond MacDonald, Gunter Kreutz, & Laura Mitchell editors
    An academically oriented, multidisciplinary collection linking music with health and wellbeing across psychology, therapy, public health, and medicine.
  • Psychology for Musicians: Understanding and Acquiring the Skills: Andreas C. Lehmann, John A. Sloboda, & Robert H. Woody
    A clear, research grounded introduction to psychological findings that matter for performers and teachers, centered on how musical skills are acquired and applied in real world music settings.
  • The Routledge Companion to Music, Mind, and Well Being: Penelope Gouk, James Kennaway, Jacomien Prins, and Wiebke Thormählen editors
    A deeper dive that situates current research in a broader history of ideas frame and includes applied angles in healthcare, education, and welfare alongside scholarship.
